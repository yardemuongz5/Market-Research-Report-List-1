Rigid substrates are typically made from materials like silicon or glass, providing stability and durability, making them ideal for high-performance applications. Flexible substrates, on the other hand, are composed of materials like polyimide, allowing for lightweight, bendable designs suited for wearable electronics and portable devices. Both types cater to distinct needs, driving innovation in various electronic applications.</p></p>
